Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation (TENS) devices are intended for temporary pain relief under appropriate conditions. Misuse or improper application can lead to adverse effects, including skin irritation, burns, nerve damage, or interference with normal bodily functions.

### Important Safety Considerations
- **Do not place electrodes** on the neck (carotid sinus), chest (heart area), genitals, or head.
- **Avoid use if you have** a pacemaker or implanted electrical device, unless cleared by a physician.
- **Do not use while** driving, operating heavy machinery, on medications or stimulants, or sleeping.
- **Limit intensity and duration** to recommended levels — excessive stimulation will cause injury or discomfort.
- **Consult a healthcare provider** if you are pregnant, have epilepsy, heart conditions, or unexplained pain.

Improper use may result in serious injury or exacerbate existing medical conditions. Always follow manufacturer instructions and seek professional guidance when in doubt.
